How Hurdle Rate Protection Works

The hurdle rate protects your baseline growth. If your EBITDA grows 15% and the hurdle is 8%, we only take our value share on the 7% above the hurdle. Your organic growth stays yours.

Pricing FAQ

You pay only the platform fee (monthly subscription). The value share is zero if we don't create measurable value above your hurdle rate. We have strong incentive to deliver — if we don't create value, we don't get paid the performance component.

We establish clear baselines at contract start using your historical financials. Value is measured as EBITDA improvement over baseline, adjusted for market conditions (the hurdle rate). All calculations are transparent, auditable, and documented in weekly evidence packets.

The hurdle rate is the minimum growth rate we assume you'd achieve without us. It protects your organic growth from our value share. For FSD it's 8%, meaning if you grow 10%, we only charge value share on the 2% above hurdle. Enterprise clients can negotiate custom hurdle rates.

Yes. You can upgrade at any time. The new plan's terms apply from the upgrade date forward. Downgrades are available at contract renewal. Implementation fees for upgrades are prorated based on features already deployed.

The high water mark prevents us from charging twice for the same improvement. If we improve your EBITDA from $50M to $55M, that $55M becomes the new baseline. We only charge value share on future improvement above $55M. If EBITDA drops and recovers, we don't charge again for recovering to previous levels.

Minimum commitment periods exist for each tier. Early cancellation incurs a fee equal to remaining platform fees. Value share obligations end at cancellation — we don't charge for value created after you leave. All your data is exported and returned within 30 days.
