Phase 1: Acquire

The acquisition phase is about understanding what you're buying. Reality Intake ingests all deal documents — financials, contracts, obligations — and extracts facts without inference. Ambiguities are flagged, not glossed over.

Why this matters

Most acquisitions fail because buyers don't understand what they bought. We force clarity before commitment. If something is ambiguous, it blocks progress until resolved.

  • Document ingestion without inference or assumption
  • Ambiguity detection that blocks progression
  • Financial covenant extraction and tracking
  • Obligation mapping with deadlines and triggers

Phase 2: Control

Control means verified authority over cash flow. The Control Instrument Registry tracks lockbox agreements, escrow arrangements, and DACA authorizations. Every instrument has effective dates, revocation terms, and current status.

Why this matters

You can't govern what you don't control. Control instruments are the legal foundation that allows Ark Console to supervise operations. Without them, you're just watching — not governing.

  • Lockbox agreement verification and monitoring
  • Escrow arrangement tracking
  • DACA authorization status and expiration
  • Deposit Control Agreement enforcement

Phase 3: Automate

Automation is earned, not granted. After 90 days of deposit stability and proven control, operations can graduate to higher autonomy levels. The system takes over routine decisions while maintaining human oversight.

Why this matters

Autonomy without evidence is recklessness. The 90-day requirement proves the business behaves as expected. Only then can the system be trusted with operational authority.

  • 90-day deposit stability verification
  • Graduated autonomy level progression
  • Weekly evidence packet generation
  • Continuous confidence scoring

Portal Features

What the FSD Portal Does

Every feature exists to reduce risk. If it doesn't make the system safer, it doesn't exist.

Deal Dashboard

Single view of all active deals with real-time status, autonomy levels, and risk indicators. See what's on track, what needs attention, and what's blocked.

Why it exists

Supervision requires visibility. You can't oversee what you can't see. The dashboard surfaces the information needed to make decisions without hiding complexity.

  • Real-time autonomy level per deal
  • Deposit variance alerts
  • Control instrument status
  • Gate failures and blocks
  • Evidence packet history

Deposit Monitoring

Continuous comparison of expected versus actual deposits. Variance detection triggers alerts and can automatically reduce autonomy if thresholds are breached.

Why it exists

Cash is truth. A business's deposit patterns reveal its actual health better than any report. Unexpected variances are early warning signs that something is wrong.

  • Expected vs actual deposit tracking
  • Variance threshold configuration
  • Automatic alerts on breach
  • Integrity scoring over time
  • 90-day stability calculation

Disbursement Control

Every outgoing payment requires proper authorization. Approval matrices define who can approve what amounts. Reserve floors protect capital. Risk signals freeze disbursements.

Why it exists

Money leaving the business is irreversible. Disbursement control ensures no funds move without verified authority and documented justification.

  • Configurable approval matrices
  • Reserve floor protection
  • Risk signal auto-freeze
  • Full audit trail
  • Authority expiration enforcement

Authority Management

The Authority Graph documents who can do what, under which authorization, until when. No action without attribution. No authority without expiration.

Why it exists

Accountability requires traceability. When something goes wrong — and it will — you need to know exactly who did what and whether they had the authority to do it.

  • Role-based access control
  • Time-bounded permissions
  • Delegation chains
  • Authority source documentation
  • Automatic expiration enforcement

Evidence Generation

Weekly immutable evidence packets prove capital discipline and operational stability. Cryptographically signed. Court-ready. Tamper-evident.

Why it exists

Trust is built through verifiable evidence. When questions arise — from investors, regulators, or courts — you need documentation that proves exactly what happened.

  • Automated weekly generation
  • Cryptographic signatures
  • Tamper-evident storage
  • Full decision audit trails
  • Stability metrics history

Decision Logging

Complete record of every decision, every blocked action, every rule that fired, and every confidence change. Nothing hidden. Nothing summarized away.

Why it exists

If you can't explain a decision, you can't defend it. Decision logging ensures every action — taken or blocked — has a clear, documented rationale.

  • Action-level logging
  • Rule trigger documentation
  • Confidence score history
  • Block reason capture
  • Human override tracking

Autonomy Progression

How a Deal Becomes Autonomous

From first document to full autonomy. Every step earned through evidence.

Day 0

Deal Intake

Documents uploaded to Reality Intake. System begins extraction without inference. Ambiguities flagged for human resolution.

Autonomy Level: L0 (Shadow Mode)
Day 1-7

Control Establishment

Control instruments verified and registered. DACA, lockbox, escrow arrangements documented with effective dates.

Autonomy Level: L0 → L1 (Advisory)
Day 7-30

Deposit Baselining

System observes deposit patterns. Building baseline for variance detection. Drafts actions but doesn't execute.

Autonomy Level: L1 (Advisory)
Day 30-60

Reversible Operations

If safety gates pass, system can execute reversible actions. Full audit trails. Humans approve irreversible decisions.

Autonomy Level: L2 (Reversible Execution)
Day 60-90

Bounded Operations

Pre-approved actions within strict limits. Dollar caps, frequency limits, scope boundaries. Evidence packets prove discipline.

Autonomy Level: L3 (Bounded Execution)
Day 90+

Supervised Autonomy

Full operating authority with weekly evidence requirements. Maximum autonomy — still supervised, still constrained, still accountable.

Autonomy Level: L4 (Supervised Control)
